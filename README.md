# warishaali_2511854_part2_kpi_experiment
The company has introduced a new onboarding and activation campaign for its subscription-based digital product and conducted an A/B test to compare it with the existing onboarding experience.

The key business decision is whether the new onboarding experience (Treatment Group) should replace the existing onboarding experience (Control Group) for all users.

This decision primarily impacts:

New users joining the platform
Product Management team
Marketing team
Customer Success team
Business leadership and stakeholders responsible for revenue growth

The primary business objective is to increase user conversion into paid subscribers while improving early user engagement. The main performance metric that should improve is the Conversion Rate, supported by improvements in user activation and retention.

However, launching the treatment without proper evaluation could introduce risks such as:

Higher customer support requests due to confusing onboarding
Increased user drop-off or bounce rates
Higher subscription cancellation rates
Technical issues affecting user experience

Before recommending a full rollout, evidence must demonstrate that:

The treatment performs better than the control on the primary success metric.
Supporting engagement metrics show improvement.
Guardrail metrics remain within acceptable limits.
The improvements are statistically and practically significant, indicating genuine business value rather than random variation.
