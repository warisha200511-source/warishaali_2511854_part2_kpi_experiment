Executive Summary

The company must decide whether to launch the new onboarding campaign to all users based on the A/B test results.

The recommendation should be based on whether the treatment group achieves a higher conversion rate and stronger user engagement without negatively affecting customer experience or business performance. Key risks such as increased support tickets, bounce rates, cancellations, and technical issues should be carefully monitored before making the final rollout decision.



North Star Metric
Conversion Rate

Definition:

Percentage of new users who successfully purchase a subscription after completing onboarding.

Why Conversion Rate is the North Star Metric

Conversion Rate directly measures the primary objective of the experiment.

The company generates revenue from paid subscriptions, making conversion the strongest indicator of business success. If more users become paying customers, the onboarding campaign successfully contributes to company growth.

Why Other Metrics are Supporting Metrics

Several metrics contribute to understanding user behavior but do not directly measure business success.

Supporting Metric	Reason
Activation Rate	Indicates onboarding completion but not payment
Day-7 Retention	Measures continued engagement after onboarding
Average Session Duration	Shows user engagement but not revenue generation
Feature Adoption	Measures product usage rather than subscriptions

These metrics help explain why conversion changes but are not the ultimate business outcome.

Connection to Business Growth

A higher conversion rate leads to:

Increased subscription revenue
Lower customer acquisition cost (CAC)
Higher customer lifetime value (CLV)
Faster business growth
Improved return on marketing investment (ROI)

Since subscriptions are the company's primary revenue source, increasing conversions has a direct positive impact on profitability.

Risks of Optimizing Only the North Star Metric

Focusing only on conversion rate could create unintended consequences, such as:

Using aggressive tactics that pressure users into subscribing.
Reducing customer satisfaction.
Increasing subscription cancellations shortly after signup.
Generating more customer support requests.
Lower long-term retention despite higher initial conversions.

Therefore, Conversion Rate should always be evaluated alongside guardrail metrics to ensure sustainable growth.
